Dataset

Though it took some trial and error, I decided to use Reddit posts as my main source of analysis for this project. Reddit offered large amounts of naturally occurring language, and the posts reflected a wide range of informal, everyday Spanish. The Dominican subreddit in particular contained a mix of Spanish, Spanglish, and occasional English, while the Mexican subreddit posts were almost entirely in Spanish. Data

The bar chart shows a clear difference in pronoun usage between Dominican and Mexican Spanish. In Dominican Spanish, t√∫ and yo are used frequently, with tu slightly higher than yo, while usted is rare. In Mexican Spanish, yo is more common than tu, and usted is almost never used (in this context).

These patterns reflect stylistic and dialectal differences. Dominican Spanish presents overt pronouns, even in casual writing, which makes the language feel more direct and personal. Mexican Spanish relies less on tu overall, most likely because informal conversation online and in person often drops the pronoun when it‚Äôs clear from context. Translation or spelling choices, regional slang, and how people write casually online also influence differences.
